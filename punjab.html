Explore Punjab

Punjab is the most populous province of Pakistan, located in the eastern part of the country.
The name “Punjab” means “land of five rivers,” referring to the rivers Jhelum, Chenab, Ravi, Sutlej, and Beas that flow through it.
These rivers make the land very fertile, which is why Punjab is often called the “food basket” of Pakistan.
The province plays a major role in the country’s agriculture, producing wheat, rice, sugarcane, and cotton in abundance.
Lahore, the capital of Punjab, is known as the cultural heart of Pakistan and is famous for its historic buildings, lively festivals, and delicious food.
Punjab has a rich and colorful culture that reflects the warmth and hospitality of its people.
The province is famous for its traditional music, energetic bhangra dance, and beautiful handicrafts.
Punjabi literature and poetry, especially the works of poets like Waris Shah and Bulleh Shah, express deep love, humanity, and devotion.
The people of Punjab celebrate many festivals such as Basant and Eid with great joy and enthusiasm.
From its fertile fields to its grand mosques and forts, Punjab represents the perfect blend of history, culture, and progress in Pakistan
