KPK

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, often called KPK, is one of the four provinces of Pakistan.
It is located in the northwestern part of the country and shares borders with Afghanistan. The province is known for its beautiful mountains, lush valleys, and rich cultural traditions.
Major cities of KPK include Peshawar, the provincial capital, as well as Mardan, Abbottabad, and Swat.
The people of KPK are mostly Pashtuns, known for their bravery, hospitality, and strong sense of honor. KPK has great historical importance because it has been a gateway for many ancient civilizations and traders.
The famous Khyber Pass has served as a trade and travel route for centuries.
The province is full of archaeological sites, ancient forts, and Buddhist ruins, showing its deep connection to history. Agriculture and tourism are two important parts of KPK’s economy.
The scenic beauty of places like Swat Valley, Kaghan Valley, and Malam Jabba attracts tourists from all over Pakistan and the world. The culture of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa is colorful and full of life. Traditional music, folk dances like the “Attan,” and local handicrafts reflect the rich heritage of the region. People wear traditional clothing such as the shalwar kameez with distinctive caps and waistcoats.
KPK is also known for its delicious food, including chapli kebab and kabuli pulao.
With its proud history, beautiful landscapes, and courageous people, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa stands as a symbol of Pakistan’s strength and diversity.
