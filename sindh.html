Explore SINDH

Sindh is one of the four provinces of Pakistan, located in the southeastern part of the country.
It is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and ancient civilization.
The mighty River Indus flows through Sindh, giving life to its fertile lands and making agriculture the backbone of its economy.
Karachi, the capital of Sindh, is the largest city and the economic hub of Pakistan.
The province is home to a mix of modern cities and traditional villages, reflecting a beautiful blend of old and new lifestyles. Sindh has a proud cultural heritage that dates back to the Indus Valley Civilization, one of the oldest in the world.
The people of Sindh are known for their hospitality, colorful clothing, folk music, and traditional Sindhi handicrafts.
Landmarks such as Mohenjo-Daro, Shah Abdul Latif Bhittai’s shrine, and the Makli Necropolis are symbols of Sindh’s historical and spiritual richness.
Sindhi cuisine, with dishes like Sindhi biryani and sai bhaji, is loved across Pakistan.
Overall, Sindh is a land of history, beauty, and deep-rooted traditions that continue to inspire generations.
